# ARRIVE — A Design Innovation Framework

Source: *Arrive: A Design Innovation Framework to Deliver Breakthrough Services, Products and Experiences* (Devitt, Ryan et al., Routledge).

## 1. The framework at a glance

ARRIVE is a six-stage *meta-process* for design innovation. The authors position it as a "choreography" that sequences design-thinking methods so multifunctional teams (especially in established firms) don't fragment into a grab-bag of disparate tools or rush prematurely to solutions.

| Letter | Stage | Core question | Primary output |
|---|---|---|---|
| **A** | Audit | What is the present state of the four innovation dimensions (user, market, technology, firm)? | Initial Project Outline; Firm's Strategic Innovation Profile; environmental scan |
| **R** | Research | Who are the users and stakeholders, and what do they really need? | Empathy maps, journey maps, ethnographic notes, raw insights |
| **R** | Reframe | What new perspective and scope makes a breakthrough solution possible? | Reframed Project Vision; Persona Needs Matrix; key insights |
| **I** | Ideate | What concepts could realise the reframed vision? | Concept Board (A); 1–2 prioritised concepts with assumptions surfaced; first-pass FVS sanity check |
| **V** | Validate | Are the critical assumptions in the concept and business model true? | Lean Canvas v1; Assumption Map; prototype-driven evidence |
| **E** | Execute | Can we prove revenue-earning capacity at limited scale before launch? | Concept Board (B); Business Case Dashboard; Execute Test Plan / Learning Experiment Canvas; updated Lean Canvas |

The four innovation dimensions — **User, Market, Technology, Firm** — are tracked across every stage. The DVFS quartet (Desirability, Viability, Feasibility, Strategic Suitability) is the success-criteria companion. **Strategic Suitability** is the authors' key extension of Tim Brown's classic D-V-F triple — a deliberate reaction to the recurring failure mode of projects that are objectively desirable, viable and feasible but die because they don't fit the firm's priorities, risk appetite, funding bands, or executive sponsorship.

## 2. The whole arc — problem framing through launch

### A — Audit (set the scene)
The fuzzy front-end most teams skip. The Audit phase tracks **three dimensions** (market, technology, firm) using classical strategy methods, deliberately deferring deep user work to Research.

Activities/decisions:
- Scope the project at a high level (Initial Project Outline).
- Map the broad ecosystem (PESTEL).
- Map the industry (Porter's Five Forces, Profit Pools).
- Identify potential sub-markets and use-cases (Customers & Noncustomers; Process Chain Mapping).
- Capture the firm's strategic posture (Mission|Vision|Values; Firm's Strategic Innovation Profile).
- Decide whether to continue to user Research now or use Verganti-style "criticism" of internal expertise as the starting point.

### R — Research (deep user understanding)
Ethnographic, qualitative, conducted in-context. "Search deeply for hitherto hidden nuggets of understanding" — not just "what" but "why".

Activities: Design Ethnography studies; Experience (Journey) Mapping; Empathy Mapping; 5 Whys; Think-Aloud Protocol.

### R — Reframe ("the secret sauce")
Pure synthesis. The new frame is *both* informed by research insight *and* defines the new scope for ideation. The Philips Ambilight TV (re-framing a TV from "viewing screen" to "central piece of living-room furniture / lighting source") is the canonical example.

Activities: Affinity Mapping → Persona → Insight Notes → Persona Needs Matrix → Reframed Project Vision → Storytelling.

The authors warn: **"innovations cannot be all things to all people"** — the frame must be the one most likely to give a compelling solution to the *main* user with the highest impact potential.

### I — Ideate (diverge, then converge to concepts)
Diverge with brainstorming, forced connections, "through-the-eyes" role-shifting, bad-idea/good-idea, metaphor & analogy. Converge by Concept Synthesis → Concept Board (A) → FVS Sanity Index.

Output: **one or two strategically outlined concepts together with their identified assumptions**. Desirability dominates here, with FVS only as a "sanity index" that catches showstoppers.

### V — Validate (de-risk the assumptions)
Concept becomes **business proposal** (covers all four dimensions). Build the first **Lean Canvas**, list assumptions on an **Assumption Map** (importance × evidence), run prototypes/experiments against the highest-risk assumptions first.

Methods: Pre-Mortem, Wizard of Oz, Role Play, Paper Prototyping, Lean Canvas, Assumption Mapping, DVFS Assertions Worksheets.

### E — Execute (prove revenue-earning capacity)
The objective is *not* to earn revenue but to *prove the capacity* to do so. MVPs at limited scale, with real paying customers if possible.

Methods: Concept Board (B) (internal-investment version), Business Case Dashboard, Execute Test Plan, Learning Experiment Canvas, ongoing Lean Canvas iteration.

Launch is a *graduation*, not a stage in itself.

## 3. Tools, canvases and templates

### Audit-stage templates

**Initial Project Outline** — single page:
- Project name and date
- Primary motivation / problem statement
- Possible areas of interest
- Initial scope boundaries (in / out)
- Known constraints
- Key questions to answer
- Success indicators (placeholder)

**Firm's Strategic Innovation Profile**:
- Strategic intent / direction
- Risk appetite (low / medium / high)
- Investment band available
- Internal capabilities (technology, channels, brand)
- Senior champion(s)
- Adjacent strategic projects
- Cultural/process readiness
- Time-to-deployment capability

This is referred to repeatedly throughout all later stages.

**PESTEL Issues + Action Map** — two-artefact pair:
- *List*: one issue per note, sorted into P/E/S/T/E/L. Each rated low/moderate/high/very high on **uncertainty** and **importance**.
- *Action Map*: 2×2 of importance vs. uncertainty, with quadrants `ACT | WATCH | SCHEDULE | IGNORE`.

**Five Forces of Competition template** — central pentagon with the firm in the middle, labelled cells for each force.

**Profit Pools template** — table mapping segments × profit share.

**Customers and Noncustomers template** — three-tier list (Blue Ocean): tier-1 soon-to-leave, tier-2 refusing customers, tier-3 unexplored.

**Process Chain Mapping template** — left-to-right value chain ribbon with sub-steps, pain points and current actors annotated.

### Research-stage templates

**Design Ethnography template** — fields for: research question, site, participants, observation notes, photos/artefacts, surprising things, emerging patterns, follow-up questions.

**Experience Mapping template** — horizontal lanes:
1. Stages of the journey
2. User actions
3. Touchpoints / channels
4. Thoughts (verbatim where possible)
5. Emotions (curve)
6. Pain points / opportunities

**Empathy Mapping template** (Dave Gray) — six quadrants: Says, Thinks, Does, Feels, Pains, Gains.

**5 Whys** — issue at top, five "why?" rows, root cause at the bottom.

### Reframe-stage templates

**Insight Note** — one card per insight: observation, underlying need, design implication, supporting quotes/evidence.

**Persona** — name, photo, demographic snapshot, behaviours, motivations, frustrations, context-of-use, key quote.

**Persona Needs Matrix** — central artefact of Reframe:
- Rows = user needs.
- Columns = priority, current solution, gap, design implication.

**Reframed Project Vision** — single page:
- Original framing (one sentence)
- New framing (one sentence — meaning shift)
- Why this frame? (insight evidence)
- Who is the focus user?
- What's in scope / out of scope under this frame
- Working title under the new frame

### Ideate-stage templates

**Concept Synthesis template** — choose a "base idea", graft in supporting ideas, name the concept, write the one-line value proposition.

**Concept Board (A)** — A3, single page:
- Concept name (evocative)
- Hero sketch / impressionistic image (low-fi deliberately)
- One-line value proposition
- 2–4 key user benefits
- Insights addressed (link back to Persona Needs Matrix)
- Headline use scenario

The book is emphatic: **low fidelity is better than near-realism**, because near-real prototypes anchor users to "is it finished?" rather than "is this idea right?".

**FVS Sanity Index** — 9 questions, 3 each under Feasibility, Viability, Strategic Suitability. Run at least monthly per live concept.

### Validate-stage templates

**DVFS Assertions Worksheet** — for each dimension (D, V, F, S) the team writes "in order for this concept to succeed, **X must be true**" assertions. Assertions are routed to the Assumption Map.

**Lean Canvas** (Maurya's 9-cell):
1. Problem
2. Customer Segments
3. Unique Value Proposition
4. Solution
5. Channels
6. Revenue Streams
7. Cost Structure
8. Key Metrics
9. Unfair Advantage

**Assumption Map** — 2×2 of *importance to success* × *strength of existing evidence*. Top-right (high-importance, low-evidence) gets prioritised for testing first.

**Pre-Mortem template** — imagined launch failure; team lists why it failed.

### Execute-stage templates

**Concept Board (B)** — internal-investment-justification version of Concept Board (A). Adds business proposition, market sizing, strategic-fit narrative, key risks, deployment plan summary.

**Business Case Dashboard** — A1 poster:
- Concept hero
- Target user / market
- Value proposition
- Lean Canvas summary
- Key validated assumptions and remaining risks
- Investment ask & expected returns
- Roll-out roadmap with learning gates

**Execute Test Plan** + **Learning Experiment Canvas** — paired:
- *Plan*: which experiments, in what sequence, against which remaining assumptions, with what budget, by when.
- *Canvas* (per experiment): Hypothesis | Method | Metric | Success threshold | Cost | Owner | Learnings.

## 4. Differences from adjacent frameworks

| Concern | Design Sprint (GV) | Lean UX / Lean Startup | Double Diamond | **ARRIVE** |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Time scale | 5 days | Continuous build-measure-learn loops | Project duration | Project duration; weeks-to-months end-to-end |
| Problem-space depth | Compressed to one day | Often skipped — starts at hypothesis | Discover diamond | Two full stages (Audit + Research) before any synthesis |
| Business viability | Light | Central via Lean Canvas | Implicit | **First-class** — DVFS quartet runs the whole way |
| Firm/organisational fit | Not addressed | Not addressed | Not addressed | **Firm's Strategic Innovation Profile** is load-bearing |
| Reframing as a stage | Implicit in HMW | Implicit in pivots | "Define" | **Dedicated stage** with its own deliverables |
| Validation type | Single Friday test | Continuous A/B and interviews | Deliver diamond | Assumption-mapped, prototype-driven, with Validate ≠ Execute |
| Best fit | Quick feature/UI exploration | Software product iterations | Generic mental model | **Established firms doing transformational, multi-dimensional innovation** |

Use ARRIVE specifically when:
- The project crosses business-model, technology and user dimensions.
- You're inside an established organisation where strategic suitability has historically killed good ideas.
- The fuzzy front end is the actual problem.
- You need an over-arching narrative that *contains* design-sprint, lean-UX and agile activities.

ARRIVE handles the multi-dimensional fuzzy front end and hands off cleanly into Lean/Agile delivery during Execute.

## 5. Examples / case studies

- **Philips Ambilight TV (2004)** — canonical reframe example: TV reframed from "viewing screen" to "central living-room furniture and ambient lighting source".
- **Elmo's Monster Maker / Monster Moves (Sesame Workshop × IDEO Play Lab)** — Wizard-of-Oz prototype built in one hour using a printed iPhone cut-out.
- **Domestic laundry** — worked Process Chain Mapping example.
- **Domestic shower enclosures** — worked Persona Needs Matrix example.
- **Airbnb** — paradigmatic assumption example: did a global accommodation company need to own its accommodation?

## 6. Critical principles

1. **Avoid solution lock-in.** The single most-repeated warning. Teams "rush prematurely towards ground that is more familiar" before properly understanding the problem space and reframing. The two-stage A+R front end exists specifically to delay this.

2. **The firm is a fourth dimension** — innovation projects fail not just on D/V/F but on Strategic Suitability.

3. **Desirability first, but never alone.** During Ideate, prioritise desirability. Run the FVS Sanity Index at least monthly so dealbreakers don't survive.

4. **Reframing is the secret sauce.** Without a deliberate Reframe stage, research becomes a deck nobody reads and ideation becomes random.

5. **Innovations cannot be all things to all people.**

6. **Prototypes are learning vehicles, not deliverables.** Low fidelity beats high fidelity at concept stage.

7. **Validate ≠ Execute.** Validation is about de-risking assumptions; Execute is about proving revenue-earning capacity at limited scale.

8. **The concept evolves into a business proposal.** Lean Canvas is the carrier.

9. **Process is a de-risking tool, not a straitjacket.** Skipping stages is allowed but **increases project risk**.

10. **Design thinking = critical thinking + growth mindset.** Without the critical-thinking half, design thinking degenerates into "flaky creativity".

11. **Human-centred means stay with the user from start to launch.**

12. **Causal vs paradigmatic assumptions** — concept assumptions ("if we build X, users will Y") get tested in Validate; paradigmatic assumptions about the status quo get tested in Reframe.

## 7. Practical adaptation — small studio, 1–2 weeks with a client

### Week 0 (pre-engagement, async, 2–3 days)
- Send the client the **Initial Project Outline** and **Firm's Strategic Innovation Profile** templates as homework.
- Run a 60-minute kickoff to walk through both.
- Async desk research: draft PESTEL, draft Five Forces, draft Customers & Noncustomers.

### Week 1 — Audit + Research + Reframe

| Day | Stage | Outputs |
|---|---|---|
| Mon AM | **Audit** | Working session: validate PESTEL Action Map, Five Forces, finalise Initial Project Outline + Firm's Strategic Innovation Profile. |
| Mon PM | **Research kickoff** | Recruit 5–6 users for Tue/Wed. |
| Tue–Wed | **Research** | 5–6 in-context interviews. Build live Empathy Maps and Experience Maps. End each day with a 30-min insight-capture using Insight Note cards. |
| Thu AM | **Reframe** | Affinity Mapping wall session (2–3 hrs). Synthesise to ≤8 Insight Notes. Build 1–2 Personas. Fill Persona Needs Matrix. |
| Thu PM | **Reframe (vision)** | Author the Reframed Project Vision. |
| Fri | **Reframe review with client** | 90-minute review meeting. Client signs off the new frame. **Biggest decision gate.** |

### Week 2 — Ideate + Validate + Execute-lite

| Day | Stage | Outputs |
|---|---|---|
| Mon AM | **Ideate (diverge)** | Brainstorm + Forced Connections + Through-the-Eyes warm-ups. Aim for 50+ raw ideas. |
| Mon PM | **Ideate (converge)** | Concept Synthesis. Pick 2 candidate concepts. First-pass FVS Sanity Index. |
| Tue | **Concept Board (A) production** | 2 Concept Boards (A) at A3. |
| Wed | **Validate (set-up)** | Lean Canvas v1. Pre-Mortem. Assumption Map. |
| Thu | **Validate (testing)** | Wizard-of-Oz, Paper Prototyping, or Role Play tests with 4–5 users. Update Lean Canvas. |
| Fri AM | **Execute-lite** | Concept Board (B), Business Case Dashboard, 2-page Execute Test Plan with 1–3 experiments scoped for the client's next 30/60/90 days. |
| Fri PM | **Final review** | Hand over the artefact pack. |

### Things that must not be cut even when compressed
- The **Firm's Strategic Innovation Profile**.
- The **Reframed Project Vision sign-off gate**.
- The **Assumption Map**.
- The **DVFS sanity check**.

### Hand-off to client
The Execute Test Plan is the bridge from your engagement to their team — design it to hand cleanly into a Lean/Agile delivery cadence.

---

## Key takeaways for skill upgrades

- **Stages and order** are non-negotiable conceptually, but execution is fractal — every method has its own diverge-converge mini-loop.
- **Artefacts travel forward** — every later-stage artefact references earlier ones (Concept Board A references Persona Needs Matrix; Lean Canvas references Concept Board A; Business Case Dashboard references Lean Canvas + Assumption Map).
- **Strategic Suitability** is the single most useful addition to import into `enterprise-ux-process`.
- **The two-R front end** (Research separate from Reframe) is the most useful addition to import into `product-discovery`.
