# Onboarding and Ramp

Ramp is the time from start date to quota-carrying performance. In SaaS it is typically 3-6 months for AEs, 6-9 for enterprise AEs, 2-3 for SDRs. Protect it. Reps thrown at accounts too early lose accounts, customers, and their own confidence.

## Ramp quota schedule (mid-market AE, $1.5M annual quota)

| Month | Ramp quota (cumulative) | % of full | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | £0 | 0% | Product, process, pitch, shadow |
| 2 | £30k | ~10% | First deals, supervised |
| 3 | £90k | ~25% | Running own pipeline |
| 4 | £180k | ~50% | Scaling, but not fully loaded |
| 5 | £280k | ~75% | Near-full load |
| 6 | £375k (full quarter) | 100% | Full quota |

Commission typically pays on actual bookings; ramp quota is the *expected* path for the purpose of attainment measurement. Many plans guarantee a "ramp bonus" in months 1-3 so the rep earns something while ramping.

## Week-by-week onboarding curriculum

### Week 1 — Foundation

- Day 1: setup (laptop, CRM, email, calendars, tools access); HR, legal, NDA.
- Days 2-3: product training — what it does, who uses it, the 3 core value stories.
- Day 4: ICP and personas — who buys, why, typical pain, competitors.
- Day 5: pitch + demo walk-through from top AE; shadowing begins.

### Week 2 — Pitch mastery

- Mornings: company deck, discovery framework (MEDDIC or SPICED), objection library.
- Afternoons: shadow 6-10 live calls (discovery, demos, closing).
- Day 5: first pitch role-play with manager. Rubric-scored (see below).

### Week 3 — Process and tooling

- CRM end-to-end (stages, fields, reports).
- Cadence tool: outbound templates, call framework, email sequences.
- Forecasting process, deal-desk flow, pricing guardrails.
- Shadow an AE's weekly forecast call and pipeline scrub.

### Week 4 — Supervised first calls

- Rep runs 3-5 discovery calls with manager or peer in the meeting.
- Structured debrief within 24 hours.
- First live demo with SE support.
- End-of-week: certified to run solo discovery.

### Weeks 5-8 — Solo with coaching

- Rep runs own pipeline; weekly 1:1 plus call review.
- Conversation intel tool surfaces coaching moments.
- Target: 2-4 active opportunities by end of week 8.

### Weeks 9-12 — First closes

- First Closed Won (target in weeks 9-12).
- First Closed Lost debrief attended by manager.
- QBR participation — rep presents their top deal publicly.

## Role-play rubric

Score 1-5 on each dimension at the end of week 2 and end of week 4:

| Dimension | What good looks like |
|---|---|
| Opening and agenda | Sets context in <90 seconds; confirms time; frames outcome |
| Discovery | Asks open questions; quantifies pain; identifies decision process |
| Listening | Does not interrupt; reflects back; 60:40 buyer talk ratio |
| Storytelling | Connects feature to pain via a customer story; avoids feature dumps |
| Objection handling | Acknowledges, clarifies, addresses; does not argue |
| Next step | Proposes clear, calendar-bound next step; confirms commitment |
| CRM capture | Notes logged same day; next step scheduled; required fields filled |

Pass bar: average 3.5, no single dimension below 3.

## Shadowing pattern

- **Week 1-2 shadow**: new rep observes 6-10 calls across AEs and deal stages.
- **Week 3-4 shadow**: new rep runs part of the call (opening, specific section); senior rep leads.
- **Week 5+ reverse shadow**: senior rep observes the new rep; manager or peer debriefs.

Keep shadowing in the schedule through month 3 for complex motions (enterprise).

## Certification gates

Do not move a rep to the next phase until they pass:

- **Gate 1 (end of week 2)**: pitch certification (role-play rubric >=3.5).
- **Gate 2 (end of week 4)**: discovery certification (live call observed).
- **Gate 3 (end of month 2)**: independent deal certification (opportunity progressed through stage 2-3 without manager in the room).

If a gate fails, extend ramp; do not hope the rep catches up on their own.

## First solo quarter

Aim to attempt full quota in the first full fiscal quarter after ramp (month 4 or 5 depending on start date). Signals the rep is on track to thrive:

- Pipeline coverage >=3x by end of ramp.
- At least one deal in Stage 3+ by end of month 3.
- First Closed Won by end of month 4 (mid-market) or month 5 (enterprise).
- Forecast calibration: commits so far within 90-110% of actuals.

Signals to intervene:

- No Stage 3+ pipeline by end of month 4.
- Pipeline generation <50% of required weekly rate.
- Consistent failure of role-play rubric on one dimension.
- Withdrawal from coaching or rejection of feedback.

Intervention pattern: document the gap in writing, specify 30-day improvement actions, meet weekly. If no progress in 30 days, plan exit.

## SDR ramp (condensed)

- Week 1: product, ICP, tools.
- Week 2: outbound framework, email writing, call opening; mock calls.
- Week 3: 30 live calls/day, with coaching.
- Week 4: full activity floor; first SQOs booked.
- Month 2-3: full quota.

## Anti-patterns

- Giving a new rep every inbound lead because "we need the number" — kills ramp quality and burns leads.
- Skipping certification gates under pressure — produces reps who never fully learn the motion.
- No ramp quota — reps feel either lazy (no target) or crushed (full target from day 1).
- "Sink or swim" — a signal that leadership cannot articulate the playbook clearly enough to teach it.
