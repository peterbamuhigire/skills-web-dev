# Roles and Specialisation

Every role exists to raise throughput or win rate at a specific point in the funnel. Specialise only when the volume or complexity of that point justifies a dedicated seat.

## Role catalogue

### SDR — Sales Development Representative (outbound)

- Mission: generate qualified meetings via cold outbound (email, calls, LinkedIn, events).
- KPIs: meetings booked, meetings held, qualified opportunities (SQO) created, activity floor (e.g., 60 touches/day).
- OTE: $60k-$90k (US); 50:50 or 60:40 base:variable.
- Quota: 8-15 SQOs/month for mid-market, 12-20 for SMB.
- When to add: you have a named-account list, AEs are spending >20% of their time prospecting, and marketing inbound alone cannot hit pipeline coverage of 3-4x quota.
- Ratio: 1 SDR per 1-2 AEs (mid-market); 1 SDR per 0.5 AE (enterprise, where more research per account is required).

### BDR — Business Development Representative (inbound)

- Mission: qualify inbound marketing leads and route to AEs.
- KPIs: speed-to-lead (<5 min for hot inbound), MQL-to-SQL conversion rate, meetings held.
- OTE: $55k-$80k; 60:40 base:variable.
- Quota: usually leads-worked or SQO target; less on activity floors.
- When to add: inbound MQL volume >200/month per AE, or speed-to-lead is collapsing win rates.

### AE — Account Executive

- Mission: run opportunities from qualified through close; own forecast.
- KPIs: new ARR booked, win rate at each stage, cycle time, pipeline coverage, forecast accuracy.
- OTE: $140k-$220k (mid-market); $250k-$400k (enterprise); 50:50 base:variable.
- Quota: 4-6x OTE (mid-market), 4-5x OTE (enterprise). Rule of thumb: quota around 5x OTE.
- When to hire: founder-led sales has closed 10+ deals with a repeatable playbook. Hire two, not one, so you can triangulate attribution.

### CSM — Customer Success Manager

- Mission: drive adoption, renewal, and expansion for an assigned book.
- KPIs: gross retention (GRR), net retention (NRR), product adoption score, QBRs completed, time-to-first-value for new customers.
- OTE: $100k-$160k; 70:30 or 80:20 base:variable (more base because relationship-led, less transactional).
- Book size: $1M-$3M ARR per CSM (mid-market); $0.3M-$1M per CSM (strategic enterprise); thousands of accounts per pooled CSM (SMB).
- When to add: NRR is flat or declining, or you have 50+ customers with inconsistent adoption. A CSM should not be hired to do onboarding alone — that is a separate onboarding specialist role at scale.

### SE — Solutions Engineer (Sales Engineer)

- Mission: technical discovery, tailored demos, proof-of-concept support, integration scoping.
- KPIs: technical win rate (share of deals where SE took the technical win), POC success rate, deals supported.
- OTE: $180k-$280k; 70:30 or 75:25 base:variable (tied to closed/won, not individually quota-carrying).
- Ratio: 1 SE per 3-4 AEs (mid-market); 1 SE per 1-2 AEs (enterprise with complex integrations).
- When to add: win rate drops after the technical-evaluation stage; POCs run longer than 30 days; AEs cannot credibly answer architecture questions.

### AM — Account Manager

- Mission: own renewal and expansion for named enterprise accounts.
- KPIs: NRR, logo retention, expansion ARR.
- OTE: $160k-$240k; 60:40 base:variable.
- Book size: 10-30 named accounts.
- When to split AM from CSM: when renewal and expansion become commercial conversations (multi-year contracts, RFPs) that need a closer, not a relationship manager. Keep CSM for adoption, AM for commercials; pair them.

## Ratios cheat sheet (mid-market org at $10M ARR)

| Role | Count | Reports to |
|---|---|---|
| AE | 5 | Sales Manager |
| SDR | 3 | Sales Manager (or SDR Manager at 6+) |
| SE | 2 | VP Sales or CTO |
| CSM | 3 | VP Customer Success |
| Sales Manager | 1 | VP Sales |
| Sales Ops | 1 | VP Sales or CFO |

## Specialisation triggers

- AEs spending >30% of time prospecting -> add SDRs.
- Inbound volume swamping AE calendars with junk -> add BDR (or BDR-lite via a router).
- Win rate drops after demo stage -> add SE.
- Post-sale adoption is chaotic, renewals are surprises -> add CSM.
- Enterprise renewals need RFP handling -> split AM from CSM.
- SDRs outnumber AEs or inverse -> rebalance; the ratio drove itself wrong.

## The POD model (van der Kooij)

Rather than organising by function (all SDRs in one group, all AEs in another), structure small cross-functional PODs: 1 manager, 2-3 AEs, 2-3 SDRs, 1 SE, 1-2 CSMs, all sharing one segment or territory, co-located (physically or virtually), and compensated partly on pod-level outcomes. The pod removes handoff friction and aligns the full customer journey around a single team. Use PODs once you have enough headcount to form at least two (otherwise it is just "the sales team").

## Anti-patterns

- Promoting your best SDR to AE without a ramp plan — different job, different skills.
- Compensating CSMs purely on NPS — they will avoid hard conversations that drive retention.
- Hiring SEs as "senior AEs who demo well" — they need architecture depth; pay for it.
- Making AMs also close new logos — split hunter and farmer unless deal volume is too small to support both.
