# The 29 Steps to Subscription Mastery

Hansen's framework, expanded into checklists with owners and deliverables. Adapt the phases to your stage — some steps repeat quarterly (pricing review, cohort segmentation), others are one-time (subscription fit, category positioning).

Phase owners below are the default for a 20–50-person SaaS. Adjust for smaller teams by having founders hold multiple roles.

## Phase A — Foundation (steps 1–7)

### Step 1 — Subscription fit

- Owner: Founder / CEO.
- Deliverable: one-page "subscription fit" memo.
- Checklist:
  - [ ] Value is delivered repeatedly (weekly or monthly), not once.
  - [ ] Customer willingness-to-pay renews with value.
  - [ ] Product ages well (or we commit to ongoing improvement).
  - [ ] Alternative is clearly worse (perpetual licence, one-off service, free).
  - [ ] Unit economics at scale survive a 2–3% monthly churn.

### Step 2 — Value promise per cycle

- Owner: CEO + Head of Product.
- Deliverable: one sentence — "Every month, our customer ___ because of us."
- Checklist:
  - [ ] Outcome-led, not feature-led.
  - [ ] Measurable (time saved, revenue added, errors avoided).
  - [ ] Tested in 5 customer interviews.

### Step 3 — Ideal customer

- Owner: Marketing / Product.
- Deliverable: 1–3 persona docs with buying triggers and disqualifiers.
- Checklist:
  - [ ] Segment-of-one description (role, company size, tool stack, problem).
  - [ ] Named anti-personas (whom we will not sell to).
  - [ ] Customer interview quotes supporting each persona.

### Step 4 — Pricing model

- Owner: CEO + Finance + Product.
- Deliverable: pricing page v1 with 3 plans and a rationale memo.
- Checklist:
  - [ ] Flat / seat / usage / tiered / hybrid chosen and justified.
  - [ ] Free / trial / paid motion decided (see `freemium-vs-trial.md`).
  - [ ] Annual vs monthly discount set (typical 15–20% annual).
  - [ ] Willingness-to-pay interviews done (5+).

### Step 5 — Positioning

- Owner: Marketing.
- Deliverable: positioning statement + competitive 2x2.
- Checklist:
  - [ ] Category named ("the X for Y").
  - [ ] Top 2 competitors and their wedge documented.
  - [ ] Differentiator phrased as a customer outcome.

### Step 6 — Brand promise

- Owner: Marketing / CEO.
- Deliverable: brand promise, tone-of-voice, and 5 supporting proof points.
- Checklist:
  - [ ] Promise is specific, measurable, enforceable.
  - [ ] Proof points are verifiable (case studies, data, SLAs).

### Step 7 — Channels

- Owner: Marketing / Sales.
- Deliverable: channel plan with 2 primary channels chosen.
- Checklist:
  - [ ] CAC tested per channel.
  - [ ] Conversion rates from first-touch to trial documented.
  - [ ] Capacity to scale channel identified.

## Phase B — Launch (steps 8–14)

### Step 8 — Landing page + offer

- Owner: Marketing.
- Deliverable: high-converting landing page (target 3–7% sign-up rate).
- Checklist:
  - [ ] Headline speaks to customer outcome.
  - [ ] Social proof above the fold.
  - [ ] Pricing visible or one click away.
  - [ ] Primary CTA tested against alternatives.

### Step 9 — Onboarding

- Owner: Product + CS.
- Deliverable: onboarding flow with time-to-first-value target.
- Checklist:
  - [ ] TTFV target set (typical 10–20 minutes for SMB SaaS).
  - [ ] Empty states replaced with starter content / templates.
  - [ ] Checklist component in-app.

### Step 10 — Activation metric

- Owner: Product + Data.
- Deliverable: single defined activation flag and dashboard.
- Checklist:
  - [ ] Cohort analysis backs the metric.
  - [ ] Instrumentation live in warehouse + CRM.
  - [ ] Target activation rate set.

### Step 11 — Payment capture

- Owner: Product + Finance.
- Deliverable: payment flow at sign-up or before trial end.
- Checklist:
  - [ ] Card capture at sign-up if paid-first, at trial-end if free-trial.
  - [ ] 3DS / SCA handled for EU customers.
  - [ ] Tax (VAT / GST / sales tax) resolved — MOR or in-house.

### Step 12 — Early-life communication

- Owner: Marketing / CS.
- Deliverable: 7–10 email week-1 sequence.
- Checklist:
  - [ ] Email 1 — welcome + first action (sent within 1 minute).
  - [ ] Emails 2–4 — activation-path reinforcement.
  - [ ] Emails 5–7 — depth and habit building.
  - [ ] Triggered on behaviour, not calendar alone.

### Step 13 — Community

- Owner: CS / Marketing.
- Deliverable: community touchpoint (Slack, forum, events).
- Checklist:
  - [ ] Place where customers see peers succeeding.
  - [ ] Moderation and on-call coverage defined.
  - [ ] Content cadence (weekly digest, monthly AMA).

### Step 14 — Customer support SLA

- Owner: Support / CS.
- Deliverable: tiered SLA and staffing plan.
- Checklist:
  - [ ] First response < 4 business hours for paid, < 1 hour for enterprise.
  - [ ] Knowledge base covers top 20 issues.
  - [ ] Ticket deflection metrics tracked.

## Phase C — Growth (steps 15–21)

### Step 15 — Habit loops

- Owner: Product.
- Deliverable: trigger/action/reward/investment mapped per use case.
- Checklist: see `engagement-loops.md`.

### Step 16 — Referrals

- Owner: Marketing.
- Deliverable: referral programme live with tracking.
- Checklist:
  - [ ] Incentive tested (account credit, month free, double-sided reward).
  - [ ] Share flow < 3 clicks.
  - [ ] Attribution end-to-end.

### Step 17 — Partnerships

- Owner: BD / Sales.
- Deliverable: 1–3 integration or reseller partnerships.
- Checklist:
  - [ ] Joint value proposition documented.
  - [ ] Co-marketing plan.
  - [ ] Revenue-share or lead-share model agreed.

### Step 18 — Content marketing

- Owner: Marketing.
- Deliverable: content calendar and SEO plan.
- Checklist:
  - [ ] Pillar pages for top-of-funnel.
  - [ ] Problem-aware and solution-aware content mapped.
  - [ ] Organic traffic target and timeline.

### Step 19 — Expansion offers

- Owner: CS / Sales.
- Deliverable: upsell and cross-sell playbook.
- Checklist: see `expansion-revenue.md`.

### Step 20 — Price increases

- Owner: CEO + Finance + CS.
- Deliverable: annual price review and migration plan.
- Checklist:
  - [ ] Grandfather vs migrate decision.
  - [ ] 60–90 day notice for existing customers.
  - [ ] Churn impact modelled.

### Step 21 — Win-back

- Owner: Marketing / CS.
- Deliverable: win-back campaign for churned accounts.
- Checklist:
  - [ ] Segmented by churn reason.
  - [ ] Re-onboarding offer (discount, new plan, assisted migration).
  - [ ] Attribution back to original cohort.

## Phase D — Retain and scale (steps 22–29)

### Step 22 — Segmentation

- Owner: Data / CS.
- Deliverable: behaviour-based customer segments.
- Checklist:
  - [ ] Segments by plan, usage, industry, maturity.
  - [ ] Health-score input per segment.

### Step 23 — Proactive churn prevention

- Owner: CS.
- Deliverable: early-warning signal stack and response SLAs.
- Checklist: see `churn-prevention.md`.

### Step 24 — Cancel flow

- Owner: Product + CS.
- Deliverable: in-product cancel flow with save offers.
- Checklist:
  - [ ] Pause, downgrade, discount options.
  - [ ] Exit survey mandatory, category + free-text.
  - [ ] Reason data piped into product backlog.

### Step 25 — Customer advisory board

- Owner: CEO + Product.
- Deliverable: CAB charter and quarterly cadence.
- Checklist:
  - [ ] 8–12 customers across segments.
  - [ ] Agenda balances listening and validating.
  - [ ] Notes published internally within a week.

### Step 26 — Financial discipline

- Owner: Finance + CEO.
- Deliverable: unit-economics dashboard.
- Checklist:
  - [ ] LTV:CAC > 3, payback < 12m.
  - [ ] NRR > 110% (SMB), > 120% (mid-market +).
  - [ ] Gross margin > 70%.

### Step 27 — Operational reviews

- Owner: COO / CEO.
- Deliverable: weekly, monthly, quarterly review rhythm.
- Checklist:
  - [ ] Weekly: pipeline + activation + support load.
  - [ ] Monthly: cohort retention + NRR + CAC by channel.
  - [ ] Quarterly: strategy, roadmap, price review.

### Step 28 — Team structure

- Owner: CEO / COO.
- Deliverable: org chart with clear ownership.
- Checklist:
  - [ ] Acquisition (marketing + sales) owner.
  - [ ] Retention (CS + support + product) owner.
  - [ ] Expansion (account management) owner.

### Step 29 — Long-term roadmap

- Owner: CEO + Product.
- Deliverable: 3-year roadmap framing evolving value.
- Checklist:
  - [ ] Extensions to existing value (more depth for power users).
  - [ ] Adjacent value (new modules, integrations).
  - [ ] New segments / geographies.
  - [ ] Platform bets (AI, automation, ecosystems).
